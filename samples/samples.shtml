The Trizbort files below weren't picked with any special algorithm. Inclusion of them isn't necessarily an indicator of quality. You can check IFDB for that. Each game is here because mapping it required some specific formatting and coloring attributes of Trizbort that are worth showing off. And because maps can give hints a straightforward walkthrough can't organize, or maybe bring back memories of critical game parts. Maps can't take the place of walkthroughs, but they can often provide people a sense of what they might've missed, or even how big a game is, or even remind them how they got through the game if they don't have the time to sit down and go through. Thanks to the authors for making these games.

Disclaimer: Andrew Schultz (who compiled this document) tested Leadlight and Escape from Summerland, helped point out a bug for Further, and have a guide at GameFAQs for Gemstone Warrior and Space Quest: the Lost Chapter.

Individual links are to PDF files, since browsers may open XML as text. The entire collection of Trizbort files is here.

Also note these are not continually updated with the latest Trizbort version.

If you have anything you think uses Trizbort in a new and interesting manner, and you'd like to share, please do leave a note. Even an issue in Github would work well. IFComp games are particularly welcome.

Leadlight (Gamma) IFComp 2010 / homepage / Itch.io Leadlight is a medium to large size game created with EAMON for an Apple II emulator, then ported to Inform 7 to use graphing capabilities. Along with region color, the map uses fills to show the existence of three types of things to find in the game: deathtraps (red), weapons (yellow) and secrets (blue). Some rooms have more than one type of thing, so colors are combined. Unfortunately, there's no good way to allow for having, say, two secrets. So the map can't do everything. But it captures where to look. Dashed borders indicate "powerball" rooms where an action can gain or lose more than one point. Dotted borders indicate rooms where you may gain two or more of a certain type of point.
Luster IFComp 2011 Luster is has a small non-reciprocal-direction maze as well as quest areas that give themselves naturally to regions. Two rooms are tagged to indicate a puzzle in one room helps with another. A one-way and dotted passage show the entrance/exit in a jail. Other formatting includes the color of gems that need to go on various pedestals near the end.
Escape from Summerland IFComp 2012 Escape from Summerland involves switching among three player characters, so color coding is used to denote where only the ghost can go, where only the monkey can go, and where the monkey and robot need to cooperate. It also features different names for the locations, since each PC sees it differently.
Spiral IFComp 2012 Spiral is another game from the same competition that features two player characters whose lives intersect at certain points only. A major goal of the game is to collect seven soul pieces for one of the players.
Further IFComp 2013 Further was an IFComp 2013 entry that used colored text to create atmosphere, and the game map attempts to replicate that. Note that even though the author didn't intend to make the player suffer with puzzles, the map may spoil the game's impact if you haven't played and want to.
Slasher Swamp IFComp 2014 Slasher Swamp features non-reciprocal exits, various regions, and several one-way exits to a certain room. One feature of the game was the necessity to search various objects, and those rooms are dashed. Numbers in the object space indicate the order to search.
Gemstone Warrior Old Apple II game

(Find it at asimov)

 Gemstone Warrior is an old Apple II action/strategy game where you go through a maze, rescue the gemstone, and get out. Trizbort's region properties and multiple exits seem suited to it.
Space Quest: the Lost Chapter Fan addition to the Sierra series Space Quest: the Lost Chapter is a huge game with many rooms that connect different ways. The second-fill feature detail how diving works as well as where guards block you off, and some colors indicate rooms you don't need to visit or should delay visiting.

There are three maps: (surface) (spaceship) (coral maze)
